Biography

Jing Sun is a final-year Ph.D. student in the School of Computer Science and Engineering (SCSE) at Nanyang Technological University Singapore (NTU, Singapore), supervised by Prof. Zhang Jie. Before joining NTU, Jing Sun was fortunate to work with Dr. Zhi Liu on machine learning (from University of Macau). He initialized her research career by mentoring and teaching her research skills.

Jing Sun's research interest mainly focuses on agent modeling in multiagent systems. I am particularly interested in leveraging deep reinforcement learning to solve challenging decision-making problems in multiagent systems with agent modeling. Furthermore, I also have a great interest in LLM-based agents and apply multi-agent reinforcement learning (MARL) algorithms to solve combinatorial optimization (CO) problems (such as vehicle routing problem).

Publications & Preprint

Decision-making with speculative opponent models.
Jing Sun, Shuo Chen*, Cong Zhang, Yining Ma, Jie Zhang,
In IEEE Transactions on Neural Networks and Learning Systems (TNNLS-2023, Accept)

Opponent modelling has proven effective in enhancing the decision-making of the controlled agent by constructing models of opponent agents. However, existing methods often rely on access to the observations and actions of opponents, a requirement that is infeasible when such information is either unobservable or challenging to obtain. To address this issue, we introduce Distributional Opponent-aided Multi-agent Actor-Critic (DOMAC), the first speculative opponent modelling algorithm that relies solely on local information (i.e., the controlled agent’s observations, actions, and rewards). Specifically, the actor maintains a speculated belief about the opponents using the tailored speculative opponent models that predict the opponents’ actions using only local information. Moreover, DOMAC features distributional critic models that estimate the return distribution of the actor’s policy, yielding a more fine-grained assessment of the actor’s quality. This thus more effectively guides the training of the speculative opponent models that the actor depends upon. Furthermore, we formally derive a policy gradient theorem with the proposed opponent models. Extensive experiments under eight different challenging multi-agent benchmark tasks within the MPE, Pommerman and StarCraft Multiagent Challenge (SMAC) demonstrate that our DOMAC successfully models opponents’ behaviours and delivers superior performance against state-of-the-art methods with a faster convergence speed.
Online adaptation with dynamic teammates in open ad hoc teamwork.
Jing Sun, Shuo Chen, Cong Zhang*, Jie Zhang.
In Proceedings of the 40th conference on uncertainty in artificial intelligence, 2024 (UAI 2024, Under Review)

Open ad hoc teamwork studies agents capable of collaborating with an unknown group of teammates that may change over time. Existing methods mainly rely on fixed and predefined teammate types, ignoring the fact that teammates may change online. This paper proposes a novel reinforcement learning method, called open online teammate adaptation framework (Open-OTAF), which allows the controlled agent to collaborate with dynamic teammates in open ad hoc teamwork. To this end, the controlled agent maintains a dual teamwork situation inference model to capture the current teamwork state for facilitating a reasonable exercise of decision-making under partial observability. Considering the dynamic types of teammates, we first leverage the Chinese Restaurant Process (CRP)-based model to categorize versatile teammate policies into distinct clusters, which improves the efficiency of identifying current teamwork situations. Next, to model the heterogeneous relationships among agents and accommodate the varying number of teammates, we model the team as a heterogeneous graph and apply the heterogeneous graph attention neural networks to learn the representation of the teamwork situation. Extensive experiments confirm that our method outperforms state-of-the-art baselines with faster convergence in various ad hoc teamwork tasks.
Solving fairness-aware heterogeneous vehicle routing problem with multi-agent reinforcement learning.
Jing Sun, Zhiguang Cao, Wen Song, Yaoxin Wu, Yining Ma*, Jie Zhang,
In Proceedings of the 30th SIGKDD Conference on Knowledge Discovery and Data Mining, 2024, 2024 (KDD 2024, Under Review)

The Heterogeneous Capacitated Vehicle Routing Problem (HCVRP), which aims to optimize routes for vehicles with diverse capacities and speeds, is crucial in logistic systems. Current HCVRP solvers, ranging from hand-crafted heuristics to the recent deep reinforcement learning (DRL) methods, often prioritize operational costs at the expense of fairness in heterogeneous fleet utilization. This paper presents FA-MARL, a pioneering multi-agent reinforcement learning approach for solving the fairness-aware HCVRP. FA-MARL promotes collaboration among multiple agents for solution construction, employing an attention-based decoder within each agent to balance individual and collective goals while incorporating fairness considerations. Additionally, it leverages a coordination-graph based fleet handler for dynamic conflict resolution during simultaneous node selection by agents. Extensive experiments on synthetic and real-world datasets demonstrate that FA-MARL not only surpasses the state-of-the-art DRL methods, but also exhibits advantages over established heuristics, notably in achieving significantly faster optimization speeds. Our study underlines the essential integration of fairness into HCVRP, representing a major advancement in creating equitable and efficient logistics solutions.
A generic framework for decision-making with speculative opponent model
Jing Sun, Shuo Chen*, Cong Zhang*, Jie Zhang,
In Artificial Intelligent. (AIJ-2023, Major Revision)

Conventional MARL methods commonly consider opponents as a part of the environment, making the controlled agents neglect the influence of other opponents, thus resulting in sub-optimal decision-making. In contrast, opponent modeling provides substantial benefits to a controlled agent’s decision-making process by constructing representative models of other agents. However, existing works typically assume access to the observations and actions of opponents, which becomes impractical when their behaviors are unobservable or difficult to acquire. In this work, we propose a Speculative Opponent Model-Aided Framework (SOMAF), a generic augmentation tool for value-based and policy-based MARL algorithms, which employs a speculated belief of the opponents to predict unknown opponents’ behaviors using local observations and make decisions accordingly. Moreover, we introduce a distributional critic to model the agent’s return distribution, which serves as guidance for the training of the agents. To demonstrate SOMAF’s effectiveness, we embed it into both policy-based and value-based MARL architectures. Comprehensive experimental evaluations substantiate that our methodology effectively models opponents’ behaviors without the requirement of their data and consistently outperforms baselines.
Optimized modeling and opportunity cost analysis for overloaded interconnected dangerous goods in warehouse operations.
Jing Sun, Fangwei Zhang, Peicheng Lu, Janney Yee.
Applied Mathematical Modelling, 90:151–164, 2021.

Storage plays an important part in the field of logistics; however, conventional storage and operation may result in an overloaded condition due to low management efficiency. To address this problem, this study proposes the concept of temporary positions in interconnected warehouses. By analyzing the opportunity costs generated, a hierarchical optimization model was constructed to quantify the improvement of temporary positions. Firstly, this study analyses the characteristics of the warehouse operation and constructs a quadratic assignment model by considering safety and efficiency. Besides, a novel genetic-BP neural network was proposed to find the optimal route of a forklift. After that, a hierarchical optimization model was constructed by considering two types of opportunity costs. Finally, an empirical study demonstrates that the interconnected mode with temporary positions has a satisfactory performance for improving operational efficiency.
Learning topological representations with bidirectional graph attention network for solving job shop scheduling problem
Cong Zhang, Zhiguang Cao, Yaoxin Wu, Wen Song, and Jing Sun
In Proceedings of the 40th conference on uncertainty in artificial intelligence, (UAI2024, Under Review)

Existing learning-based methods for solving job shop scheduling problem (JSSP) usually use off-the-shelf GNN models tailored to undirected graphs and neglect the rich and meaningful topological structures of disjunctive graphs (DGs). This paper proposes the topology-aware bidirectional graph attention network (TBGAT), a novel GNN architecture based on the attention mechanism, to embed the DG for solving JSSP in a local search framework. Specifically, TBGAT embeds the DG from a forward and a backward view, respectively, where the messages are propagated by following the different topologies of the views and aggregated via graph attention. Then, we propose a novel operator based on the message-passing mechanism to calculate the forward and backward topological sorts of the DG, which are the features for characterizing the topological structures and exploited by our model. In addition, we theoretically and experimentally show that TBGAT has linear computational complexity to the number of jobs and machines, respectively, which strengthens the practical value of our method. Besides, extensive experiments on five synthetic datasets and seven classic benchmarks show that TBGAT achieves new SOTA results by outperforming a wide range of neural methods by a large margin.
B-spline estimation of single index model with missing data
Jianbo Li, Jing Sun,
In Chinese Journal of Applied Probability and Statistics, 2019, 35(5):525–534, 2019
This paper mainly studies the inverse probability weighted estimation problem of a single indicator model based on random missing response variables. First, the unknown single indicator function is approximated by B-spline, and then the inverse probability weighted least squares loss function is constructed, and then the indicator is obtained through a two-stage Newton iteration algorithm The estimation of function and index coefficients, and finally the effectiveness and rationality of our proposed estimation method are illustrated through a large number of simulation examples and case analysis.
Hesitant distance set on hesitant fuzzy sets and its application in urban road traffic state identification
Fangwei Zhang, Jihong Chen, Jianbo Li, Jing Sun, and Augustine Attey.,
In Engineering Applications of Artificial Intelligence, 61:57–64, 2017

Since fuzziness lacks the distinction between a set and its complement, it is difficult to measure the distance between different hesitant fuzzy sets (HFSs) by a single value. In this study, a new concept “hesitant distance set (HDS)” is proposed, where the distance between different HFSs can be characterized by a series of different values. This study has three primary contributions. Firstly, most of the existing distance measures on HFSs are based on vector operation, while the novel proposed HDSs are based on set operation. Secondly, a statistical method is proposed to compare different HDSs, and some important properties of the comparison method are introduced. Thirdly, the characteristics of the novel HDSs and the classical hesitant distances are studied comparatively. Finally, the practicality and validity of the HDSs on HFSs are illustrated through an urban road traffic state identification example.

Based on comparative studies on correlation coefficient theory and utility theory, a series of rules that utility functions on dual hesitant fuzzy rough sets (DHFRSs) should satisfy, and a kind of novel utility function on DHFRSs are proposed. The characteristic of the introduced utility function is a parameter, which is determined by decision-makers according to their experiences. By using the proposed utility function on DHFRSs, a novel dual hesitant fuzzy rough pattern recognition method is also proposed. Furthermore, this study also points out that the classical dual tool is suitable to cope with dynamic data in exploratory data analysis situations, while the newly proposed one is suitable to cope with static data in confirmatory data analysis situations. Finally, a medical diagnosis and a traffic engineering example are introduced to reveal the effectiveness of the newly proposed utility functions on DHFRSs.
Novel parameterized distance measures on hesitant fuzzy sets with credibility degree and their application in decision-making
Jiaru Li, Fangwei Zhang Qiang Li Jing Sun, Janney Yee Shuhong Wang , and Shujun Xiao
In Symmetry, 10(11):557, 2018.

The subject of this study is to explore the role of cardinality of hesitant fuzzy element (HFE) in distance measures on hesitant fuzzy sets (HFSs). Firstly, three parameters, i.e., credibility factor, conservative factor, and a risk factor are introduced, thereafter, a series of novel distance measures on HFSs are proposed using these three parameters. These newly proposed distance measures handle the relationship between the cardinal number and the element values of hesitant fuzzy set well, and are suitable to combine subjective and objective decision-making information. When using these functions, decision makers with different risk preferences are allowed to give different values for these three parameters. In particular, this study transfers the hesitance degree index to a credibility of the values in HFEs, which is consistent with people’s intuition. Finally, the practicability of the newly proposed distance measures is verified by two examples.
